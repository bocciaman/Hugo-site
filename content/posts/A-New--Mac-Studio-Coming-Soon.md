Back in July I finally got a new desktop Mac that will be my daily driver for the foreseeable future: a 2023 Mac Studio. After years of working across various Macs, I wanted a machine that could be the rock-solid hub for everything I do — the automation projects, the video work for the channel, the writing, and the general day-to-day. The Mac Studio felt like exactly the right call, and I wanted to share the full setup and my reasoning behind each choice.

## Why the Mac Studio?

For the kind of work I do, the Mac Studio hits a sweet spot that the other Macs don't quite reach. It packs serious desktop-class power into a compact box that sits quietly on the desk, and crucially, it's not a laptop — I wanted a dedicated machine that lives at my workstation and is always ready to go, rather than something I'm constantly docking and undocking. The Mac mini was tempting on price, but I wanted more headroom; the Mac Pro is overkill (and over-budget) for my needs. The Studio splits the difference perfectly.

## What the Specs Mean in Practice

Let me explain the choices, because they weren't random. The **M2 Max with a 12-core CPU and 38-core GPU** is genuinely overpowered for a lot of what I do day to day, but it's exactly what you want when you start editing video for YouTube — exporting and timeline scrubbing are where that GPU and those cores earn their keep, and a faster machine means less time staring at progress bars and more time creating.

The **32GB of unified memory** is the comfortable middle ground for my workflow. Unified memory on Apple Silicon is shared efficiently between the CPU and GPU, so 32GB handles a stack of open apps, a browser full of tabs, and a video project at the same time without breaking a sweat. The **2TB SSD** was a deliberate splurge — video files are enormous, and I'd rather have the local storage headroom than constantly juggle external drives. And that **10Gb Ethernet port** is a quiet luxury for moving large files around my network quickly.

I also went with the **Magic Keyboard with Touch ID in an Arabic layout**, which is a nice personal touch — Touch ID on a desktop Mac is genuinely convenient for unlocking and approving things without typing a password every time.

## The One Regret: The Monitor

I'll be honest about the weak link in this setup: the monitor. I paired the Studio with a **Dell S2422HZ**, and in hindsight it's not a purchase I'd repeat. It's perfectly serviceable and gets the job done for now, but it doesn't do justice to a machine this capable — a Mac Studio really deserves a sharper, higher-quality display to match. It'll do for the time being, but upgrading the monitor is firmly on my list for the next couple of years. Consider this my gentle word of advice: don't skimp on the display when you've invested in a great machine, because you stare at the screen far more than you think about the box under the desk.

## Coming from My Previous Setup

Moving to the Mac Studio was a real step up from what I'd been working on before, and the difference shows up in the small moments more than the benchmarks. Apps launch instantly, heavy tasks don't bog the whole system down, and — because it's a fanless-quiet desktop under most loads — I can actually concentrate without a jet-engine noise reminding me the machine is working hard. The Apple Silicon efficiency is genuinely impressive: it does more work while staying cooler and quieter than the Intel Macs I'd used in the past. For anyone still on an older Intel Mac and wondering whether the jump to Apple Silicon is worth it, my honest answer is an emphatic yes; it's the kind of upgrade you feel every single day, not just during demanding tasks.

## Looking Ahead

This Mac Studio is going to be the engine behind everything I share here and on the Automation Maestro channel for a long time to come. A fast, reliable, always-on desktop changes how you work — there's no waiting for it to wake up, no thermal throttling under load, just a quiet machine that's ready whenever inspiration strikes. I'm excited to put it through its paces, and you'll see the results in the automation projects, videos, and tutorials to come. If you're weighing a Mac Studio yourself, I hope this rundown helps you think through the configuration that's right for you.
