## What TextExpander Actually Does

If you're new to the idea, here's the core concept. You create a short "abbreviation" and assign it a longer block of text — your "snippet." Then, anywhere you type on your Mac, iPhone, or iPad, typing that abbreviation instantly expands it into the full text. Type `;addr` and your full mailing address appears. Type `;sig` and your email signature drops in. Type a couple of characters and a whole paragraph of boilerplate materializes. It works system-wide, in any app, which is what makes it so much more powerful than the canned-response feature buried inside any single program.

The reason it's such a game-changer is that we all type the same things far more often than we realize — addresses, greetings, replies to common questions, dates, links, code we reuse. Each instance only costs a few seconds, but multiply that across a day, a week, a year, and TextExpander gives you back a genuinely meaningful amount of time. Just as importantly, it eliminates typos and inconsistency, because your snippets are always spelled and formatted exactly right.
