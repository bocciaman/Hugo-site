Tired of bloated apps for tracking your weight loss and reading? In this post, I walk through 2 custom iPhone Shortcuts I built to log my books and my weight — straight into the Notes and Apple Health apps. No subscriptions, no clutter, just simple automation that lives on your phone.

These two shortcuts replaced multiple tracking apps for me, and they're tied to my iPhone Action Button for one-tap logging.

## Shortcut #1: Book Reading Log

The **Book Reading Log** shortcut creates an organized note per book with the title, author, and pages read — then automatically appends new entries when you continue reading. No more hunting through separate apps or losing your reading history. Everything lands neatly in Apple Notes, organized and ready to review.

📥 **[Download the Book Reading Log Shortcut](https://www.icloud.com/shortcuts/7ffaa3eb2a4a4f5f825e882bb081e46d)**

## Shortcut #2: Weight Loss Diary

The **Weight Loss Diary** shortcut asks for your weight in pounds, converts it to kilograms, logs it directly to Apple Health, and saves a dated entry in Notes. One tap from your Action Button and you're done — no app to open, no account to sign in to.

📥 **[Download the Weight Loss Diary Shortcut](https://www.icloud.com/shortcuts/5e81038245684e2a82067d78a4f567b6)**

## Why Use Shortcuts Instead of Apps?

Most tracking apps want a subscription, your data, or both. iPhone Shortcuts run locally, sync through iCloud, and integrate with the apps already on your phone — Notes, Health, Reminders, and more. They're free, fast, and fully customizable to how you actually live.
