## What Is Alfred, and Why Upgrade?

For anyone new here: Alfred is a productivity launcher for the Mac, a supercharged replacement for Spotlight. At its simplest it lets you launch apps and search your Mac with a couple of keystrokes, but with the Powerpack it becomes an automation powerhouse — custom workflows, clipboard history, text snippets, and far more. It's one of the cornerstone apps in my Mac setup.

Alfred 5 brought a round of refinements and new capabilities over Alfred 4, and like any tool you rely on every day, it's worth keeping current so you get the latest features and fixes. The good news is that upgrading doesn't mean starting from scratch — your workflows and preferences carry over.

## The Upgrade Process, Step by Step

Here's exactly what I do in the video. The whole thing breaks down into three simple stages.

**First, buy the license.** Alfred 5 is a paid upgrade, so you'll grab a license from the Alfred website. Depending on when you bought your previous Powerpack and which license type you hold, your upgrade pricing may vary, so it's worth checking what applies to you before you purchase.

**Second, install the Alfred 5 application.** Download it and move it into your Applications folder like any other Mac app. When you launch it for the first time and enter your Powerpack license, Alfred picks up your existing setup — your workflows, snippets, and preferences come along for the ride, so you're not rebuilding anything.

**Third — and this is the critical part — remove Alfred 4 the right way.** Once you've confirmed Alfred 5 is installed and working, you'll want to get rid of the old version. Here's the key warning: **delete Alfred 4 using the Trash only.** Just drag it to the Trash and empty it.

## The One Mistake to Avoid

I want to underline this because it's the single most important takeaway from the video: **do not use an uninstaller app like AppCleaner when removing Alfred 4 during an upgrade.**

Normally I'm a big fan of AppCleaner for thoroughly removing apps and all their leftover files — I've recommended it many times. But this is the exception. The reason is that Alfred 5 shares and inherits some of Alfred 4's supporting files and preferences. If you blast everything away with an uninstaller, you risk wiping out the very settings and workflow data that Alfred 5 needs to carry over your configuration. You could end up losing customizations you spent ages building. So during an Alfred upgrade specifically, resist the urge to "deep clean" — the simple Trash method is exactly what you want, because it leaves the shared support files intact for Alfred 5 to use.

## What You Get in Alfred 5

It's worth knowing what you're upgrading *to*, because Alfred 5 isn't just a version-number bump. The release brought a meaningfully better workflow editor — the canvas where power users build their automations — with conveniences like a built-in "Automation Tasks" library of pre-made actions you can drop into workflows without scripting, easier debugging, and visual touches that make complex workflows far more manageable. There were refinements to the core search experience too, plus the usual round of performance and reliability improvements that come with a major version. If you lean on Alfred for more than just launching apps, these workflow-building upgrades are the real reason to make the jump rather than sitting on version 4 indefinitely.

## After the Upgrade

Once Alfred 4 is gone and Alfred 5 is running, take a few minutes to confirm everything made the jump. Open your workflows and check they're all present, test a couple of your most-used snippets, and make sure your hotkey and preferences feel right. In my experience everything transfers cleanly, but it's always reassuring to verify before you move on.

## Conclusion

Upgrading to Alfred 5 is a quick, low-stress process as long as you remember the golden rule: install the new version, confirm it works, and remove the old one with the Trash — never an uninstaller. Do it that way and all your hard-won workflows and settings come right along with you.

If you're ready to make the jump, you can purchase Alfred 5 directly from the [Alfred website](https://www.alfredapp.com). It remains one of the best tools on my Mac, and version 5 only makes it better.
