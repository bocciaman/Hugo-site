## The Problem: A Desktop That Becomes a Dumping Ground

Be honest — how does your desktop look right now? For most of us, it's where files go to be forgotten: screenshots pile up, downloads land there, images and documents scatter across it until the wallpaper is barely visible. A cluttered desktop isn't just an eyesore; it slows you down every time you have to hunt for the file you actually need, and on the Mac it can even nibble at performance since every desktop icon is a live preview. The trouble is that *keeping* it clean takes constant manual effort — and that effort never sticks. The mess always creeps back.

That's exactly the kind of repetitive, willpower-draining chore that automation is born to handle, and it's why I reach for Hazel.

## What Hazel Does

Hazel is a file-monitoring app for the Mac. You point it at folders and give it **rules**, and from then on it watches those folders and automatically acts on files based on conditions you set — their type, age, name, source, and more. It runs quietly in the background, so the tidying happens on its own without you ever thinking about it. Instead of cleaning your desktop, you teach Hazel how *you* want it cleaned, once, and it maintains that order forever.

## My Three-Pronged Folder Approach

In the video, I demonstrate the exact system I use to keep my own desktop spotless. It's built around a simple staging-and-archiving idea:

- **Desktop Clutter Folder** — instead of letting files sit on the desktop, Hazel sweeps them into a dedicated clutter folder, sorted by type:
	- **Screenshots** — keeps screenshots separate for a month
	- **Images** — keeps images separate for a month
	- **Docs** — keeps documents separate for a month
- **Google Drive Folder** — after files have lived in the clutter folder for a month untouched, Hazel automatically moves them here for long-term storage.

All of this happens automatically through Hazel rules. The beauty of the system is the time-based handoff: recent files stay close at hand in the clutter folder (neatly categorized so you can still find them), and anything you haven't needed in a month gets archived off to Google Drive without you lifting a finger. Your desktop stays clean, nothing gets lost, and old files quietly migrate to where they belong.

## Why I Like This Setup

What makes this approach work is that it doesn't *delete* anything — it just keeps things moving along a sensible path. You get the satisfaction of a pristine desktop without the anxiety of throwing files away. And because it's all rule-based, it scales effortlessly: drop a hundred files on your desktop and Hazel sorts every one of them by the rules you've already defined. You can adapt the same idea to your own life — different categories, different timeframes, a different archive destination like a NAS or external drive.

## Beyond the Desktop

While I built my system around the desktop, that's really just the beginning of what Hazel can do — and once you see the pattern, you'll want rules everywhere. The classic place to point Hazel next is your **Downloads folder**, which for most people is an even bigger dumping ground than the desktop: you can have Hazel sort PDFs into a documents folder, images into a pictures folder, and installers into the trash after they've been used. You can also have it automatically rename files into a consistent format, tag them with colors so you can spot categories at a glance, or move finished projects into an archive after a set period. Because rules trigger on conditions you define — file type, age, name, even the website a download came from — you can build a quietly self-maintaining filing system across your whole Mac. Start with one folder, get comfortable, and expand from there.
