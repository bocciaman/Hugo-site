## The Problem: One Default Browser Isn't Enough

Here's a frustration most of us just learned to live with: macOS lets you pick exactly one default browser, and every single link you click anywhere — in Mail, Messages, Slack, Twitter, a PDF — opens in that one browser, whether it's the right tool for the job or not. But in real life, we don't use browsers that way. Maybe you like Safari for general browsing because it's fast and battery-friendly, but your work tools only behave properly in Chrome. Or you keep a separate browser for a specific account. With a single default, you're constantly copying a link, switching browsers by hand, and pasting it in. It's a small thing that happens dozens of times a day.

That's the exact problem Velja solves, and it's why it earned a permanent spot on my Mac.

## What Velja Does

Velja is a free menu-bar utility that lets *you* decide which browser opens which link. Instead of macOS blindly sending everything to your default, Velja steps in and gives you the choice — or makes the choice for you based on rules you set. In the video, I show this in action: I opened an Apple link from Twitter in Safari even though Safari wasn't my default browser at the time. Velja gave me that option on the spot, no copying and pasting required.

The real power, though, is in the rules. Velja lets you set up **per-domain rules** so the right browser is chosen automatically. In another example from the video, I set up a rule so that any Zendesk link opens in Chrome — automatically, every time, without me lifting a finger. I don't have to launch Chrome first, copy the link, and paste it in; Velja just routes it where it belongs. You can build as many of these rules as you like: work domains to one browser, personal sites to another, specific apps to a third. Velja essentially acts as a smart traffic cop for your links, handing your freedom of choice back to you.

## Why I Love It

A few reasons this little app punches above its weight. First, it's invisible until you need it — it sits quietly in the menu bar and only speaks up when a link is clicked. Second, the rules turn what would be a manual decision into pure automation; once you've told Velja that Zendesk goes to Chrome, you never think about it again. And third, it respects how people actually work in 2020s macOS, where different sites and services genuinely behave better in different browsers. It's the kind of tool that fixes an annoyance you'd half-forgotten you had, and then you can't imagine going back.

## Where to Get It

Velja can be downloaded from [Sindre Sorhus](https://sindresorhus.com/velja), which will take you to its App Store page. Sindre is a well-known and prolific Mac developer behind a whole catalog of genuinely useful free utilities, so you're in good hands. Setup is quick: install it, grant it the permission to handle links, and start adding your rules.

## Free vs. Paid Alternatives

For most people, Velja is all you'll ever need, and I found it to be the best **free** option available — which is hard to beat. If you want something with even more advanced routing features, there are paid alternatives like [Choosy](https://choosy.app/), which runs around $10. Choosy is powerful and worth a look if you have especially complex link-routing needs, but honestly, for the vast majority of users Velja covers the job beautifully without costing a thing.

## A Few Ways I Use It

To make this concrete, here are a few rules I find genuinely useful day to day. Work tools that only behave in Chrome — like certain admin dashboards or support systems — get a rule so they always open there, while everything else stays in Safari for speed and battery life. Links I want to read later but not in my main browser can be routed to a separate browser I use purely for reading. And when I'm not sure where a link should go, I leave it ruleless so Velja simply asks me, giving me a quick picker instead of a wrong guess. You can get as granular or as hands-off as you like — the tool scales to however much control you want over your links.

## Conclusion

If you've ever been annoyed that every link opens in the "wrong" browser, Velja is the fix. It gives you back control over where your links open — manually when you want to choose, or automatically through per-domain rules when you don't want to think about it. It's free, it's lightweight, and it solves a real, everyday friction. Grab it from Sindre Sorhus's site, set up a couple of rules, and enjoy having your browser freedom back.
